Photography

Advertising Food Photography

Simple and clean, graphic-meets-real-world environment with minimal propping.  Shoot with bold color surfaces and textures. Authentic food styling for appetite appeal; sweet spot between reality and stylized. Strong light source to give life and pop. Get creative with different angles and configurations for a bolder, more dynamic food presentation. Match the energy of the spots.

Guidelines

Attainable, Chef-Inspired Flavor, Bold Meal Combinations & Shareability,Bite-Sized Fun

Attainable, Chef-Inspired Flavor

When a menu item has layers of ingredients or complex flavor combinations, we’ll use heroic angles and macro shots, to highlight the chef-inspired flavor and convey a quality message. The background will be bold, but won’t compete with the food.

Bold Meal Combinations & Shareablilty

To showcase variety and choice, we’ll feature multiple menu items at once. If it’s a hero or classic menu item, we will depict a variety of complementary food behind it, indicative of how Cravers actually order and think about mealtime.

Bite-Sized Fun

When a menu item is typically ordered in multiples, or is extra portable, we want to convey a fun, poppable flair. We’ll use shots that contain elements of humanity and depict the food within context of its portable nature or quantity served. This can help spotlight the value message visually while standing out from everyone else.

Social Voice

Our voice is casual, informal and unique. We have a distinct personality that our fans crave. We like to keep it real and think outside the (slider) box. Because we all know that your Twitter timeline is really just a treadmill for your thumb. So, let’s always give Cravers something worth stopping for—from the OG slider to cheese wands, er, mozzarella sticks. Basically, we never pretend to be something we’re not. And we know exactly who we are—the famous film director, Wes Cravin'. OK, kidding about that last one.

Social Tone

Our tone is witty, likeable and upbeat (in a clever, not sappy way. Sap is for trees, not the interwebs).  Will we encourage our Cravers to join us in a chorus of “Crinkle Crinkle Star” whilst fry-eating? Maybe. But only if #NationalNurseryRhymeDay is trending. Or if we’re feeling extra singsongy. Will we tweet a photo of 20 golden Chicken Rings in honor of that champion-swimmer-that-we-can’t-legally-mention winning 20 gold medals in the big international games that occur every 4 years? You betcha.We’re all about serving up a good time. Preferably with a side of circley Onion Rings.  And a slider or four.
